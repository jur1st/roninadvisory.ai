# Mockup A — Editorial Masthead

**Thesis.** The front page of Ronin Advisory Group is the cover of a quarterly the firm publishes, and the firm's services are its colophon — because a consultancy that reads as an editorial practice reads as one that thinks on the record.

The move Mockup A is making is a category claim. Most professional-services sites are brochures that imitate the shape of SaaS landing pages — a hero statement, a three-column benefits grid, a testimonial strip. That shape is wrong for forensics work. Evidentiary competence is not bought on the same plane as a productivity tool; buyers of it are looking for signals that the practitioner thinks carefully, publishes their reasoning, and is not in a hurry. The editorial-masthead framing supplies those signals before any copy is read. The name sets at display scale in FreightDisp Pro, slightly negative-tracked, with an italic Group mark tucked up at the shoulder the way a legal suffix sits on a title page. The services appear as a numbered colophon — italic oxblood folios, grotesque labels, a hairline rule between each entry — because presenting them as a colophon quietly asserts that this is where the engagements are recorded, not pitched. Gunnerbench is not a marketing artifact on this page; it is an inked card in the foot position marked "N°01," framed as the first issue of a continuing periodical. The thesis of the firm and the thesis of the publication collapse into the same gesture.

What was rejected matters as much as what was kept. There is no hero section, no call-to-action button, no KPI tile, no gradient, no stock imagery, no phone number (by requirement, and no substitute). The palette is two inks on warm paper — a deep warm black and an editorial oxblood — with a half-step surface tone reserved for later supporting pages. The type system has one serif family at two optical sizes (FreightDisp for the cover voice, FreightText for body) plus one grotesque (Acumin Pro) carrying every piece of structural matter. Three voices is the ceiling; a future editor adding a fourth face is probably building a different page. The double-rule inside the trim is a printed-border convention faked with a CSS `border` plus an `outline` at offset; the rule-work SVG in `assets/` is a mid-century triple rule with a centered diamond, used once between the foot and the imprint line. The warm off-white paper tone (not the blue-leaning `#fafafa` the project scaffold ships with) is load-bearing: this direction collapses the moment the page feels sterile.

A future editor extending this design without breaking its premise should keep three things in mind. First, the company name is the logotype — there is no mark in reserve, and adding one (a katana, a kanji, a stylized R) will read as costume and undo the seriousness the rōnin framing is carrying. Second, the colophon structure is the site's spine; interior pages (engagements, writing, Gunnerbench issues) should continue to render as typeset matter — numbered, ruled, set in the grotesque for structure and the serif for prose — not as cards or tiles. Third, oxblood is the only non-ink color; a second accent added for a CTA or a status badge will shift the register from editorial to commercial, and at that point the theory of the brand has been replaced by a different theory. The design is recoverable from those three rules, even after the copy grows and the file count doubles.
