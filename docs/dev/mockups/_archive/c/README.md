# Mockup C — The Seal

**Thesis.** Ronin Advisory Group is a firm where one person signs every report, and the front page should read like that signature — a single hand-cut mark, bounded, deliberate, sufficient.

The mark is the theory of the brand. A *rōnin* is not a soldier in search of a lord; he is a technician under his own authority, and that authority is what a client hires. So the mark refuses everything that would dilute it. No helmet. No katana. No circle-and-character lockup. It is a seal — the kind a craftsman etches on the back of a case — and its vocabulary is four strokes inside a square: a **frame** (the engagement has declared scope, open at the top-right because the operator's departure is also declared), a **vertical** (the operator's bearing, standing on the floor of the frame without touching its ceiling), a **crossbar at the upper third** (resolve, placed as a decision rather than a midpoint), and a **witness notch** at the lower-left (evidence that the seal was cut by hand, not stamped). It is meant to survive reduction — legible at 24px as a favicon, commanding at 240px on the page, still itself at print size on a letter of engagement. It deliberately does *not* mean: martial arts, Japan-as-aesthetic, outlaw romance, or any of the Bushido-as-brand gestures the category encourages. If a future editor wants to evolve it, the premise to preserve is that **the four strokes are the whole argument**; anything added is ornament, and ornament is what the mark exists to reject.

Everything else on the page is scaffolding for the mark. Body is Berkeley Mono because a firm that writes forensic reports should speak in the voice of a report — measured, fixed-width, unshowy. The display anchor is Helvetica Neue LT Com (condensed for the nameplate, regular for practice-area names) because a neutral grotesque is the right foil for the mark: it carries the firm's name without competing. Palette is near-monochrome on warm paper (`oklch(0.96 0.010 85)`) — the off-white of a case folder, not the blue-white of SaaS — with a single accent (oxidized iron, `oklch(0.43 0.135 28)`) used once, on the link that goes out. I rejected a serif body (too editorial; it would have made the firm read as a magazine), a cooler paper (too clinical; it would have made the page read as a login screen), and a framed "logo lockup" alongside the wordmark (redundant; the mark already *is* the lockup). Services render as an indexed list (01–04) rather than cards because cards-in-a-grid is the exact shape this direction is trying to avoid — a list with rules is what a competent operator's work product looks like on paper. Gunnerbench is surfaced as a dated masthead strip under its own small nameplate rather than a featured card; the parent brand is the firm, and the periodical is a thing the firm does. A future editor who needs to add pages (*engagements*, *writing*, *about*) should hold the line that the mark never shares the hero with anything else — it is either alone at the top of the page, or it is absent and implied.
